Seated Shoulder Squeeze

It is the foundation of the shoulder joint and holds it in place on the body.

How do you do seated shoulder squeezes?


  • Sit on the floor with your knees bent and feet flat on the floor.

  • Clasp your hands behind your lower back.

  • Straighten and extend your arms and squeeze your shoulder blades together.

  • Do this for 3 seconds, and then release. Repeat 5 to 10 times.

What are the benefits of seated shoulder squeezes?

  • This exercise is beneficial for shoulder and upper limb stability for major movements performed everyday such as pushing, pulling and holding items

  • It is also specifically prescribed to improve posture and avoid ‘rounded’ shoulders.

  • This exercise is one of the best, when it is about training the muscles between the shoulder blades without equipment
